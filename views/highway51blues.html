Highway 51 was a popular topic in blues recordings made in the earlier half of last century. It's a dark path travelled by a forlorn narrator in a do-or-die search for a departed sweetheart. Dylan's excursion down this forsaken road exhibits both blues and rockabilly influences, drawing most of its lyric content from bluesman Tommy McLennan's "New Highway 51 Blues" and its musical arrangment from the Everly Brothers' "Wake Up Little Suzy".

Bob Dylan -- Highway 51 Blues

Borrowing one from the Elvis Presley playbook, Dylan takes a delta blues tune and gives it a fernetic rockabilly makeover sans backing combo. Just as Presley transformed Arthur Crudup's "That's Alright,Mama" on his debut recording session, Dylan applies an analogous process to his source material here. The guitar part sounds like a sped-up version of the one heard on the Everly Brothers hit "Wake Up Little Susie" and perhaps served as a groove template for his later classic, "That's Alright Ma, I'm Only Bleeding".

In an earnest,resourceful effort to sound like anything but a middle-class Jewish kid from Minnesota, Dylan tries out a smorgasbord of "roots" vocal stylings -- okie twang, Delta growl, etc. -- and fashions a mashup that effectively serves the lyrics after all.

In addition to the verses 1 and 3 taken from Tommy McClennan's "New Highway 51" recorded in 19xx for the Bluebird Label, verse 2 is an apparent Dylan original that is idiomatic,deft and loosely autobiographical. This verse evidences Dylan's precocious appreciation for the haiku-like semantics and jagged,assymetrical phrasing characteristic of blues lyrics. It is an open question why Curtis Jones was given songwriting credits: his doomy,elegant "Highway 51 Blues" bears no resemblance to the Dylan version.

Like solo,rural blues performers, Dylan dispenses with the strictures of the conventional 12-bar, 4/4 blues form, opting for a fluid conversation between voice and guitar. His barrel down Highway 51 maintains the groove and respects the common AAB blues lyric form but ignores all traffic lights and sometimes weaves into the opposite lane.
